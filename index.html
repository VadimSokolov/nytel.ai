The coordination problem

Medidata, Veeva, and IQVIA have improved their platforms. But the work that lives in the seams between systems — chasing documents from sites, reconciling data, tracking follow-ups — remains a manual job.

Document collection

Site coordinators submit CVs, training certificates, medical licenses, and regulatory forms via email. Tracking what's missing, what's expired, and what needs correction is a full-time job.

5 TMF documents cover 80% of QC burden

Quality checks

Every document must meet ALCOA++ standards, GCP training currency, signature verification, and sponsor-specific rules. Manual review is slow and inconsistent.

80-90% of checks are universal across sponsors

Follow-up and escalation

When sites don't respond, the escalation ladder runs from coordinator to field monitor to country monitor to study lead — over 21 days of manual tracking.

$1M/day in enrollment delay costs

Staff turnover

Median tenure in clinical operations is 1.5 years. Every departure takes institutional knowledge — which sites are slow, which sponsors have unusual requirements — with it.

Built for regulated environments

Nytel sits above your existing clinical systems as an orchestration layer — it reads state from CTMS, eTMF, and EDC and writes actions back, but does not replace them.

Deterministic where it matters

Quality checks use code and rules, not generative models. Every decision is auditable and reproducible. LLMs handle document understanding, not compliance logic.

Human-in-the-loop

OCR confidence below 0.80 triggers human review. Safety exceptions skip the escalation ladder. Study teams can pause, override, and flag sites as quiet.

Email-first integration

Phase 1 requires no API credentials, no IT procurement, no changes to partner systems. Direct API integration follows once workflows are validated.

Sponsor-configurable

Universal QC rules cover 80-90% of checks. Sponsor overrides — stricter thresholds, custom templates, additional fields — configure on top without touching the rule engine.
